everything around you

Every Bluetooth device within ~10 meters is continuously broadcasting its presence. Your phone. Their phones. Headphones. Watches. Speakers. Fitness trackers. Medical devices. Every single one is sending packets into the air right now — whether they're connected to anything or not.

this is how retail stores track your path through the building. Fixed BLE scanners at each aisle record your phone's MAC address as you pass. Your dwell time at each product is logged. No app required on your end. The Jida scooter app we reversed earlier uses the same principle — except their DEVICE_KEY was hardcoded globally, so any scooter is unlockable.

▶ how does this work?

Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) devices advertise themselves on channels 37, 38, and 39 (2.402, 2.426, 2.480 GHz). They send advertisement packets up to 10 times per second containing their MAC address, device name, signal strength (RSSI), and optional manufacturer data.

Web Bluetooth API on Chrome/Android lets a webpage scan for these broadcasts with user permission. No pairing required. We just listen.

Signal strength (RSSI) is in dBm. -40 dBm = very close. -90 dBm = edge of range. Each 6 dBm difference ≈ 2× distance change.
